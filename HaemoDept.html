Haemotology Department

Hematology, also spelled haematology, is the branch of medicine concerned with the study of the cause, prognosis, treatment, and prevention of diseases related to blood. It involves treating diseases that affect the production of blood and its components, such as blood cells, hemoglobin, blood proteins, bone marrow, platelets, blood vessels, spleen, and the mechanism of coagulation. Such diseases might include hemophilia, blood clots (thrombus), other bleeding disorders, and blood cancers such as leukemia, multiple myeloma, and lymphoma.

Our comprehensive service includes non-malignant haematology, malignancy, haemostasis/thrombosis, haemophilia We also take care of blood disorders such as anaemia, acute and chronic leukaemia myelodysplastic and myeloproliferative disorders, lymphoma, multiple myeloma. Our patient centered approach to the delivery of care is further enhanced by an integrated haematology day care facility where chemotherapy may be delivered in an ambulatory setting in addition to blood product transfusion, central venous catheter management, growth factor/cytokine administration and other aspects of supportive care.
